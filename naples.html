Naples

Naples is the capital of the region of Campania. It is the third largest city in Italy after Rome and Milan. The city has origins as an ancient Greek colony and is rich with history and culture. It has a population of over 960,000 inhabitants, and more than 4.4 million if you count the wider metropolitan area.

With its coastal proximity, Naples also boasts proximity to the beautiful Capri and Amalfi Coast, as well as only an hour away from the historic site of Pompeii.

Top 3 Things to do in Naples

A muse for writers, an inspiration for artists and a refuge from the noise of the world, Capri is an idyllic island surrounded by impossibly blue sea.

Pompeii was an ancient Roman city that was buried alive in the enormous volcanic eruption of Mount Vesuvius in 79 AD. Discover its fascinating history!

Built when Naples was under Spanish rule, the Palazzo Reale is home to lavishly decorated royal apartments, a lavish theatre and a chapel.
