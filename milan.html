Milan

Milan located in Northern Italy is a global capital of fashion and design. Home to the national stock exchange, it’s a financial hub also known for its high-end restaurants, fashion and culture. The Duomo di Milano cathedral and the Santa Maria delle Grazie convent, housing Leonardo da Vinci’s mural “The Last Supper,” testify to centuries of art and culture.

Located centrally in the north, the city is only a few hours away from the beautiful Lake Como, Genoa Coastline or the stunning city of Venice. For this reason, this is a must visit destination.

Top 3 Things to do in Milan

Milan Cathedral, or Metropolitan Cathedral-Basilica of the Nativity of Saint Mary, is the cathedral church of Milan, Lombardy, Italy. It is an architecturally stunning and iconic building with free entry. There is however a small fee to enter the rooftop and museum below.

he Last Supper is a mural painting by the Italian High Renaissance artist Leonardo da Vinci. Located at the Santa Maria delle Grazie, this is a must see, however tickets sell out fast so book well in advance.

Lake Como, in Northern Italy’s Lombardy region, is an upscale resort area known for its dramatic scenery, set against the foothills of the Alps. This lake is well-regarded as one of the most beautiful lakes in the world, and in only a few hours by train from Milan. A must see for those seeking more natural wonders.
